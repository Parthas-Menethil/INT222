World Capitals

The capital city is essential to a country. It’s like the central headquarter which gathers national significant institutions, and usually takes a key role of a country’s polity and economy. In the modern world, efficiency may determine a nation’s destiny. With important people and departments in the same city will reduce the time of their interaction; as a result it can reduce the time of making decisions as well as taking measurements. It is sensible to have a unique capital city in a country.

Canada

Ottawa is located in south east of Ontario next to Quebec. The Ottawa River goes through the city and separates it into two parts. Later generations of French live in the north part of Ottawa and speak French. In contrast, people in the south with English ancestors speak English. With landmarks such as Rideau Canal and the National Gallery of Canada, it attracts visitors from all around the world every year.

Japan

As the largest city in Asia and one of the most developed cities in the world, Tokyo is an important global finance, economy and technology centre. It has been the capital city of Japan since 1960s and the urban infrastructures are well developed. Although during the development of Tokyo the appearance of the city varies through ages, Tokyo still keeps a significant amount of historical relic, sites as well as traditional ceremonies, and it becomes a unique pattern of Tokyo.

China

Beijing is one of Chinese acient cities and has a history of more than 3000 years since it was constructed as a city. With the world's most amount of World Heritages in one city, Beijing has the Forbidden City, the Great Wall and many other acient sites. They attract millions of people every year.

America

Washington D.C. Is the world's most powerful nation – the United States of America's capital city. It is the United States' economic and cultural centre and is only for administrative functions which is not usual in the world. To administrate the nation properly is the main job of Washington D.C.. It was first named Columbia in honour of the land founder Cristoforo Colombo, but was changed by American Congress to Washington in memory of the first president of America – George Washington.
